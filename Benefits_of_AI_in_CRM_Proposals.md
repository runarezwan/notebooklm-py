## Option 1: Operational Efficiency & Speed
### Title: Quantifying the Operational Benefits of AI Integration: A Comparative Study of Loan Processing Turnaround Time (TAT) in Bangladeshi NBFIs

*   **Problem Statement:** Manual loan assessment is slow, leading to customer dissatisfaction and high operational costs. The CRM workflow at IPDC faces delays during document verification and manual data entry.
*   **Research Aim:** To measure and analyze the reduction in Turnaround Time (TAT) achieved through AI-driven automation in the CRM department.
*   **Research Objectives:**
    1.  Map the current manual CRM workflow to identify "High-Latency" stages.
    2.  Implement (or simulate) an AI-based automated document parsing system.
    3.  Compare the TAT of manual assessment versus AI-assisted assessment.
    4.  Estimate the cost-savings per loan application after AI integration.
*   **Theoretical Framework:** Technology-Organization-Environment (TOE) Framework.
*   **Methodology:** Quantitative (A/B testing or historical data comparison) + Cost-Benefit Analysis.

## Option 2: Consistency & Decision Quality
### Title: The Benefits of Algorithmic Standardization: Reducing Subjective Bias and Improving Decision Consistency in IPDC's CRM Department

*   **Problem Statement:** Human credit assessment is often subjective. Different officers may reach different conclusions on the same loan application, leading to portfolio inconsistency and potential risk.
*   **Research Aim:** To investigate how AI-driven "Standardized Assessment" improves the quality and consistency of credit decisions.
*   **Research Objectives:**
    1.  Identify common subjective factors that influence human credit decisions at IPDC.
    2.  Develop a predictive "Consistency Model" using historical loan data.
    3.  Evaluate how an AI "Second Opinion" impacts the final approval rate.
    4.  Measure the correlation between AI-assisted decisions and lower loan default rates.
*   **Theoretical Framework:** Diffusion of Innovation (IDT) Theory.
*   **Methodology:** Mixed Methods (Analyzing historical loan performance data + Surveys with Senior Credit Officers).

## Option 3: Business Growth & Financial Inclusion
### Title: Expanding the Portfolio: Assessing the Benefits of AI-Driven Alternative Data Analysis for SME Credit Growth at IPDC Finance

*   **Problem Statement:** IPDC wants to grow its SME portfolio, but many small businesses lack the formal financial records required by manual CRM processes.
*   **Research Aim:** To explore how AI can benefit IPDC by enabling the assessment of "Credit-Invisible" SME segments using alternative data sources.
*   **Research Objectives:**
    1.  Identify sourceable alternative data (Utility bills, mobile money, supply chain transactions) for Bangladeshi SMEs.
    2.  Analyze how AI models (Random Forest/XGBoost) can predict creditworthiness from this non-traditional data.
    3.  Estimate the potential market size increase if IPDC adopts AI-driven SME assessment.
    4.  Develop a risk-benefit framework for AI-led financial inclusion.
*   **Theoretical Framework:** Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT).
*   **Methodology:** Quantitative Modeling & Market Opportunity Analysis.
