## 1. Introduction
Leadership is not just about leading other people. It starts with leading yourself. This is called **Self-Leadership**. It means using your own thoughts and actions to reach your goals (Manz & Sims, 2001). In this report, I will look at my personality test results. I am an **ENTJ (Commander)**. I will explain how my personality helps me lead myself and others. I will also talk about my experience in school, work, and family life.

## 2. My Personality: The Commander (ENTJ)

### 2.1 My Test Results
The 16Personalities test says I am an **ENTJ-A (Assertive Commander)**. Here are my main traits:
*   **Intuitive (74%)**: I like to think about the future and new ideas. I do not like boring details.
*   **Judging (90%)**: I really like to plan and organize everything. I like to make fast decisions.
*   **Thinking (70%)**: I focus on facts and results. I do not always think about feelings first.
*   **Extraverted (59%)** and **Assertive (74%)**: I get energy from other people. I am confident and do not get stressed easily.

As a "Commander," I am good at making a plan and getting things done. I like to take charge and give order to messy situations (16Personalities, 2024). But focus on results (70% Thinking) can sometimes mean I forget about people’s feelings.

### 2.2 My Strengths and Weaknesses
My biggest strengths are making **quick decisions** and **organizing projects**. I always want to reach the goal. For example, in my MBA studies, I often lead the group to make sure we finish our work on time. 

My weaknesses are that I can be **too critical** of others. I also find it hard to **delegate** (give work to others) because I want everything to be perfect. Because of my "Judging" trait (90%), I can get impatient when things move slowly. I need to learn to be more patient.

## 3. Using Self-Leadership Theory
Self-leadership has three main strategies: behavior, natural rewards, and thought patterns (Neck & Houghton, 2006).

### 3.1 Behavior Strategies
I am good at **Self-Goal Setting**. I always have a target. But I need to improve my **Self-Observation**. I should watch my own behavior more closely. Sometimes my drive for speed makes me act too bossy. If I observe myself, I can stop before I hurt someone’s feelings (Manz, 1986).

### 3.2 Natural Reward Strategies
This strategy is about making work fun. For me, the fun is usually in "winning" or finishing a task. I need to find "natural rewards" in helping my team grow, not just in finishing the work. This will help me balance my focus on results with a focus on people.

### 3.3 Thought Pattern Strategies
I am a very confident person because of my **Assertive (74%)** trait. My "self-talk" is positive. But I should practice visualizing success *with* my team. I should think "We can do this together" instead of just "I can do this" (16Personalities, 2024).

## 4. My Leadership Experience

### 4.1 In Work and Studies
I have seen that my "Commander" style works well in a crisis. But in normal days, it can be too intense for my team. For example, during a volunteer project, I was upset because others were moving slowly. Now I know I should have used **self-cueing** to remind myself to listen to them more.

### 4.2 In My Community and Volunteer Work
In my community activities, I have learned that leadership is often about **valuing different strengths**. In a volunteer group, everyone has a different way of working. My need for speed and quick results can sometimes make people feel their way is not good enough. I have learned that being a leader in this setting is not just about the fastest solution. Sometimes it is about understanding that everyone has unique talents and ways of contributing. This is an important part of my growth.

## 5. My Plan for Growth

To be a better leader, I will work on three things:

1.  **Emotional Intelligence (EI)**: I will stop and listen before I speak. I will think about how the other person feels first. 
2.  **Delegation**: I will trust my team more. I will give them important tasks and let them do it their own way. This is a big change for me (Neck & Houghton, 2006).
3.  **Rest and Relaxation**: I am very ambitious and often take on too much work. I will make a goal to rest and relax more so I do not get too tired.

## 6. My Future as a Leader
In the future, I want to lead a team that is both smart and happy. I do not want to be just a boss who gives orders. I want to help others grow. My goal is to use my strategic mind (74% Intuitive) to solve big problems, but use my self-leadership skills to be a kind and fair leader.

## 7. Conclusion
The 16Personalities test shows I am an ENTJ "Commander." I am strong, decisive, and like to plan. However, to be a great leader, I need to use **Self-Leadership**. I must learn to be more empathetic and trust my team. If I can lead myself first, I can lead others to success in a better way. As I study at XAMK, I will keep working on these skills every day.
