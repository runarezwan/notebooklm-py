## 1. Introduction & Background
The rapid proliferation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) has created a significant "digital divide" between large corporations and Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs). While large enterprises possess the capital to hire dedicated AI teams and data scientists, SMEs often find the barrier to entry—high initial consulting fees, specialized recruitment costs, and hardware/software investments—insurmountable. This creates a market gap for a new service delivery mechanism (OECD, 2021).

Traditional AI consultancy is frequently project-based and capital-intensive. This research proposes the development of a **Subscription-Based AI Agency Model** (Fractional AI-as-a-Service). This model aims to democratize AI access by providing ongoing, cost-effective, and scalable AI internal expertise to SMEs through a recurring service model, rather than a one-time high-cost project.

## 2. Problem Statement
Many SMEs recognize the potential of AI but lack the liquidity and internal maturity to execute full-scale integration (Keränen, 2025). Existing consultancy models often focus on high-value, high-complexity projects that are tailored to larger firms, leaving SMEs with "off-the-shelf" solutions that may not address their specific operational workflows. There is a lack of a structured, sustainable, and cost-effective business model that provides continuous AI governance and implementation support specifically designed for the financial and organizational constraints of an SME.

## 3. Research Aim & Objectives
This research aims to **develop and validate a subscription-based business model for an AI Agency** that specifically targets the cost-effective adoption of AI in SMEs.

**The specific objectives are:**
1.  To identify the primary financial and organizational barriers to AI adoption in SMEs.
2.  To analyze existing service models (consultancy, SaaS, etc.) and their limitations for small enterprises.
3.  To design a subscription-based AI Agency model (value proposition, pricing tiers, and service delivery).
4.  To evaluate the perceived feasibility and cost-effectiveness of this model from the perspective of SME owners.
5.  To provide a strategic implementation guide for future AI service providers.

## 4. Research Questions
1.  How can a subscription-based agency model lower the barrier to AI adoption for SMEs?
2.  What core services (e.g., fractional AI leadership, automation, training) offer the highest value-to-cost ratio in a subscription model?
3.  How can the model ensure long-term scalability and sustainability for both the agency and the SME client?
4.  What are the key success factors of a "Fractional AI CDO" service within the SME ecosystem?

## 5. Theoretical Framework
This study will combine business model innovation with technology adoption theories:

*   **Design Science Research (DSR):** The primary framework used to design and evaluate the "artifact" (in this case, the business model). DSR focuses on solving practical problems through structured creation and validation.
*   **Service-Dominant Logic (S-D Logic) (Vargo & Lusch, 2004):** To analyze the value co-creation process between the agency and the SME.
*   **Business Model Canvas (Osterwalder & Pigneur, 2010):** To map the nine building blocks of the proposed agency model.
*   **Transaction Cost Economics (TCE):** To justify why a subscription model (repeated interactions) is more cost-effective for SMEs than one-off market transactions (standard consultancy).

## 6. Research Methodology
### 6.1 Research Approach
A **Constructive Research Approach** within the **Design Science Research (DSR)** paradigm will be used. This involves defining the problem, designing a solution (the model), and validating it through expert feedback.

### 6.2 Data Collection
*   **Expert Interviews:** Semi-structured interviews with 3-5 SME leaders and AI consultants to gather requirements for the model.
*   **Model Validation:** A second round of interviews or a focus group to present the drafted model and evaluate its perceived cost-effectiveness and feasibility.

### 6.3 Data Analysis
*   **Thematic Analysis:** To extract requirements and validation markers from the interviews.
*   **Business Model Comparison:** Benchmarking the new model against traditional AI implementation costs.
