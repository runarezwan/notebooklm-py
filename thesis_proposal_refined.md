## 1. Introduction & Problem Statement
Artificial Intelligence (AI) offers SMEs transformative potential for automation and decision-making (Brynjolfsson & McAfee, 2017). However, adoption remains low due to resource constraints and skill gaps (OECD, 2021). In the **Kymenlaakso region**, SMEs face specific hurdles in digital maturity. This study, aligned with the **GoGlo project**, addresses the lack of empirical research on AI adoption in regional SMEs, aiming to bridge the "digital gap" by identifying local readiness and specific barriers.

## 2. Research Aim, Objectives & Questions
The aim is to evaluate AI adoption among Kymenlaakso SMEs, focusing on opportunities, challenges, and organizational readiness.

**Objectives:**
1.  Assess the current level of AI adoption.
2.  Identify AI-driven business opportunities and value propositions.
3.  Explore internal/external challenges to adoption.
4.  Evaluate organizational readiness for AI implementation.
5.  Provide strategic recommendations for SMEs and the GoGlo project.

**Research Questions:**
*   What is the current status of AI adoption in Kymenlaakso SMEs?
*   What strategic opportunities does AI provide for regional competitiveness?
*   What critical barriers (TOE factors) hinder AI integration?
*   How ready are SMEs in the region for AI-driven transformation?

## 3. Theoretical Framework
An integrated lens will be used to analyze adoption:
*   **TOE Framework (Tornatzky & Fleischer, 1990):** Technical, organizational, and environmental factors.
*   **IDT (Rogers, 2003):** Innovation diffusion patterns and perceived attributes.
*   **RBV (Barney, 1991):** Internal resource/capability assessment for competitive advantage.

## 4. Methodology
*   **Approach:** Qualitative research using semi-structured interviews.
*   **Data Collection:** Interviews with 3–5 SMEs in Kymenlaakso (AI adopters or planners). Supplemented by a short regional survey if feasible.
*   **Analysis:** Thematic analysis (Braun & Clarke, 2006) to identify readiness patterns and barriers.
