## 1. Introduction and Background
As the financial sector in Bangladesh undergoes rapid digital transformation, Non-Banking Financial Institutions (NBFIs) like IPDC Finance are increasingly integrating Artificial Intelligence (AI) to streamline operations. The **Credit Risk Management (CRM)** department, responsible for the critical task of loan appraisal and approval, is at the forefront of this change. 

While AI offers the potential for high-speed data processing, its implementation often creates a **socio-technical gap**. Credit officers—experts with years of intuitive risk-assessment experience—may perceive AI as a "black box" that threatens their professional autonomy or job security. For a digital industry to be sustainable, AI must be designed as a **collaborative tool (augmented intelligence)** rather than a replacement (automated intelligence). This research focuses on bridging this gap within the specific organizational context of IPDC Finance.

## 2. Problem Statement
The primary barrier to AI adoption in IPDC’s CRM department is not technological capability, but **user trust and role shift**. Credit officers are often hesitant to rely on AI-generated recommendations because they do not understand the underlying logic, leading to "algorithm aversion." Furthermore, there is a lack of a structured framework that defines how an AI assistant should be integrated into the CRM workflow without causing cognitive overload or professional resistance. Without a human-centered design approach, IPDC’s investment in AI may fail to deliver the expected operational gains due to low employee engagement. This study addresses the absence of localized, human-centric research in the Bangladeshi NBFI sector.

## 3. Research Aim and Objectives
The aim of this research is to investigate the socio-technical impact of AI on the role of Credit Officers at IPDC Finance and to develop a **Human-Centered AI (HCAI) Framework** for collaborative loan assessment.

**Specific Objectives:**
1.  To explore the current perceptions, fears, and expectations of IPDC CRM officers regarding AI integration.
2.  To identify the specific user experience (UX) and interface design needs of financial experts when interacting with AI tools.
3.  To analyze the shift in the "Credit Officer" role from a manual analyst to an **AI-augmented decision-maker**.
4.  To develop a design framework for an "AI Assistant" that enhances human expert capabilities while maintaining human oversight.
5.  To provide strategic recommendations for IPDC Management on change management and digital culture in a high-stake department.

## 4. Research Questions
1.  How does the introduction of AI affect the professional identity and decision-making processes of Credit Officers at IPDC Finance?
2.  What design features (transparency, feedback loops, etc.) increase trust and usability in an AI-based CRM assistant?
3.  How can the CRM workflow be re-engineered to facilitate effective **Human-AI Collaboration**?
4.  What are the key organizational factors in the "Digital Industry" context that support successful AI adoption in financial services?

## 5. Theoretical Framework
This research will utilize three pillars of modern digital industry theory:
*   **Socio-Technical Systems (STS) Theory (Trist & Bamforth, 1951):** To understand the interplay between the "Social" (the people) and the "Technical" (the AI) in the workplace.
*   **Human-Centered AI (HCAI) (Shneiderman, 2022):** Focusing on high levels of human control and high levels of computer automation to empower people.
*   **Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT) (Venkatesh et al., 2003):** To evaluate user readiness and the strategic factors influencing the behavioral intention to adopt AI.

## 6. Research Methodology
### 6.1 Research Approach
A **qualitative research approach** will be employed, as it allows for a deep exploration of the subjective experiences, strategic motivations, and emotional responses of specialized financial professionals.

### 6.2 Data Collection
*   **Semi-structured Interviews:** Primary data will be collected through in-depth interviews with 5–8 CRM officers at IPDC Finance across different seniority levels.
*   **Participatory Design Prototyping:** A small-scale session where officers provide feedback on wireframes or conceptual designs of an AI dashboard.
*   **Document Analysis:** Reviewing existing CRM workflow manuals to identify friction points and automation opportunities.

### 6.3 Data Analysis
*   **Thematic Analysis:** Interview transcripts and workshop notes will be analyzed to identify recurring patterns regarding adoption hurdles, trust factors, and desired AI functionalities.

## 7. Expected Outcomes
*   A localized **Socio-Technical Framework** for AI adoption in Bangladesh's financial sector.
*   Strategic UX/UI guidelines for **Explainable AI (XAI)** in loan assessment tools.
*   Organizational recommendations for IPDC Finance to manage the digital workforce transition.
