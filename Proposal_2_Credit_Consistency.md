# Thesis Proposal 2: Credit Consistency & Bias

**Title:** The Benefits of Algorithmic Standardization: Reducing Subjective Bias and Improving Decision Consistency in IPDC's CRM Department  
**Department:** CRM, IPDC Finance Ltd.  

## 1. Introduction
Credit risk assessment is often treated as an "art" rather than a "science" in many Bangladeshi financial institutions. While experience is valuable, it leads to subjective decisions where two different credit officers might give different ratings to the same borrower. This inconsistency increases the risk of Non-Performing Loans (NPLs).

## 2. Problem Statement
Human decision-makers are prone to "Cognitive Bias"—factors like the time of day, the officer's mood, or personal intuition can lead to varied credit outcomes. This lack of standardization makes it difficult for IPDC to ensure a uniform risk appetite across the organization. There is a critical need for an AI-based "Decision Support System" to act as a standardized benchmark.

## 3. Research Aim & Objectives
The aim is to evaluate how AI standardization improves the reliability of credit decisions.
1. To identify recurring patterns of subjective bias in historical CRM assessments.
2. To design an AI framework that provides a "Standardized Risk Score" as a second opinion.
3. To measure the reduction in "Decision Variance" between different credit officers when using AI tools.
4. To correlate AI-recommended decisions with loan repayment success rates.

## 4. Research Questions
1. To what extent does subjectivity cause variance in credit decisions at IPDC?
2. How does an AI "Second Opinion" influence the final decision-making of a human credit officer?
3. Can algorithmic standardization lead to a statistically significant reduction in NPLs?

## 5. Methodology
**Mixed-Methods Approach:**
*   **Quantitative:** Analyzing historical credit files to find inconsistencies.
*   **Qualitative:** Interviews with senior approvers to understand their "intuitive" decision-making logic and comparing it to AI logic.
