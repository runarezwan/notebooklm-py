# Thesis Proposal 1: Turnaround Time (TAT) & Efficiency

**Title:** Quantifying the Operational Benefits of AI Integration: A Comparative Study of Loan Processing Turnaround Time (TAT) in Bangladeshi NBFIs  
**Department:** CRM, IPDC Finance Ltd.  

## 1. Introduction
In the competitive financial landscape of Bangladesh, speed is a strategic asset. Customers often choose a financial institution based on how quickly their loan is approved. IPDC Finance aims to be the fastest in the market, but its current CRM assessment process remains slowed down by manual verification steps. This research explores how AI-led automation can optimize these workflows.

## 2. Problem Statement
The current manual Turnaround Time (TAT) for SME and retail loans at IPDC is subject to significant delays due to manual document extraction, physical file movement, and human-led verifications. These delays lead to high operational costs and "Customer Churn" (customers leaving for faster banks). There is no empirical study at IPDC that quantifies exactly how much time can be saved by integrating AI at specific CRM stages.

## 3. Research Aim & Objectives
The aim is to measure the efficiency gains from AI integration in the CRM lifecycle.
1. To identify stages in the CRM flow with the highest latency.
2. To simulate an AI-driven workflow for document parsing and verification.
3. To compare the TAT of the manual vs. AI-assisted process.
4. To calculate the potential increase in "Application Throughput" (number of apps processed/month).

## 4. Research Questions
1. Which specific stages of the IPDC CRM process contribute most to delays?
2. By what percentage can AI-led automation reduce the total TAT?
3. What is the projected ROI (Return on Investment) for IPDC in terms of operational man-hours saved?

## 5. Methodology
**Quantitative Approach:**
*   **Time-Motion Study:** Measuring exactly how many minutes/hours officers spend on manual tasks currently.
*   **Simulation/Modeling:** Using historical data to model an AI-process and comparing it to the baseline manual process.
