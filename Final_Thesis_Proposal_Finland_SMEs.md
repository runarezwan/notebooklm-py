## 1. Introduction and Background
Artificial Intelligence (AI) is fundamentally transforming global business operations by enabling process automation, sophisticated data-driven decision-making, and enhanced competitive positioning. In the Finnish context, Small and Medium-sized Enterprises (SMEs) are vital economic drivers, yet their adoption of AI technologies remains uneven. While the benefits of AI are recognized, Finnish SMEs often struggle with a systemic lack of digital skills, financial constraints, and limited strategic awareness regarding AI’s practical applications (Keränen, 2025; Khan et al., 2025).

The **Kymenlaakso region** presents a unique landscape for digital transformation. Regional development initiatives, such as the **GoGlo project**, are actively working to bridge the digital gap and enhance the global competitiveness of local enterprises. Recent Finnish-based research highlights that organizational readiness, data maturity, and perceived value are the primary determinants of successful AI integration (Skromnov, 2025). This study seeks to investigate these factors specifically within the Kymenlaakso SME ecosystem.

## 2. Problem Statement
Despite the accelerating pace of AI development, SMEs in Kymenlaakso encounter significant barriers that prevent them from fully leveraging these technologies. These obstacles include inadequate internal expertise, a lack of structured knowledge regarding AI’s potential, and severe resource limitations (Maddumage & Madhushani, 2025). Furthermore, there is a notable absence of empirical research specifically focused on the Kymenlaakso region, leaving local policymakers and business leaders without a clear understanding of the region’s specific challenges and opportunities. This research aims to fill this critical gap.

## 3. Research Aim and Objectives
The primary aim of this research is to examine the adoption of Artificial Intelligence in Kymenlaakso-based SMEs, focusing on the identification of opportunities, challenges, and the assessment of organizational readiness.

**The specific objectives are:**
1. To assess the current status and depth of AI adoption among SMEs in Kymenlaakso.
2. To identify the specific business opportunities AI provides for regional competitiveness.
3. To explore the primary technological and organizational barriers faced by these SMEs.
4. To evaluate the level of organizational readiness required for sustainable AI implementation.
5. To provide evidence-based recommendations for SMEs and regional support programs like GoGlo.

## 4. Research Questions
1. What is the current level of AI adoption among SMEs operating in the Kymenlaakso region?
2. What are the key strategic opportunities that AI offers to enhance the internationalization of Finnish SMEs?
3. What are the most significant challenges and barriers hindering AI adoption in the regional context?
4. How prepared are Kymenlaakso-based SMEs—in terms of resources and culture—for AI implementation?

## 5. Theoretical Framework
This research utilizes a multi-dimensional framework to analyze the factors influencing AI adoption in the Finnish SME sector:

*   **Technology-Organization-Environment (TOE) Framework (Tornatzky & Fleischer, 1990):** This will be used to analyze the interplay between technological characteristics, internal organizational structures, and the external regional environment.
*   **Innovation Diffusion Theory (IDT) (Rogers, 2003):** This perspective helps explain the patterns of AI adoption and how perceived complexity and relative advantage affect the speed of integration.
*   **Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT) (Venkatesh et al., 2003):** Applied in various Finnish SME studies, UTAUT will be used to evaluate user readiness and the behavioral intention to adopt new AI tools.

## 6. Research Methodology
### 6.1 Research Approach
A **qualitative research approach** will be employed, as it allows for a deep exploration of the subjective experiences and strategic motivations of business leaders.

### 6.2 Data Collection
*   **Semi-structured Interviews:** Primary data will be collected through interviews with 3–5 representative SMEs in Kymenlaakso.
*   **Small-scale Survey (Optional):** If feasible, a brief descriptive survey will be used to gather broader regional insights and establish baseline trends.

### 6.3 Data Analysis
*   **Thematic Analysis:** Interview transcripts will be analyzed using thematic analysis to identify recurring patterns regarding adoption hurdles and success factors.
*   **Descriptive Statistics:** Survey data will be summarized to illustrate regional trends in readiness and AI tool usage.

## 7. Expected Outcomes
*   Comprehensive insights into the regional AI adoption landscape.
*   Identification of industry-specific barriers and opportunities for Kymenlaakso SMEs.
*   Practical readiness assessment and strategic recommendations for the GoGlo project.
