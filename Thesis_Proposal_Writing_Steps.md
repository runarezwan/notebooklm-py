# Step-By-Step Guide: Writing Your Master’s Thesis Proposal
**Focus:** Digital Industry & AI Applied to Finance (IPDC Case Study)  

This guide outlines the full 10-step process to get your thesis proposal approved by your supervisor and the management at IPDC.

---

### Step 1: Define the Problem (The "Pain Point")
Before you write anything, identify what is actually "broken" in the CRM process.
*   *Action:* Talk to 2-3 credit officers. Ask: "What is your biggest daily headache? Is it a slow process, messy data, or hard-to-follow rules?"
*   *Goal:* Your proposal must solve a real business problem.

### Step 2: Conduct a Preliminary Literature Search
You need to show that you aren't the first person to think of this.
*   *Action:* Search Google Scholar or ResearchGate for: *"AI in Banking Bangladesh," "Automation in NBFI,"* or *"Human-AI trust in finance."*
*   *Goal:* Find 5-10 academic sources to back up your proposal.

### Step 3: Draft your Research Questions (RQ)
Your RQs determine your entire thesis. They must be specific.
*   *Bad RQ:* "Can AI help IPDC?" (Too vague).
*   *Good RQ:* "How does the implementation of an AI-driven RAG system impact the document verification time for SME loan applications?" (Measurable).

### Step 4: Choose your Methodology
This is the "How" of your research.
*   **Qualitative:** Use this if your topic is about *people’s feelings* or *processes* (Interviews, workshops).
*   **Quantitative:** Use this if your topic is about *speed, accuracy, or money* (Data analysis, coding, math).

### Step 5: Select a Theoretical Framework
Academic supervisors love frameworks. It proves you are using scientific methods.
*   *Recommended for you:* **TOE Framework** (Technology-Organization-Environment) or **UTAUT** (Acceptance of Technology).

### Step 6: Secure Data/Company Permission
Because you are working with IPDC, you need to handle sensitive data safely.
*   *Action:* Discuss with your HOD at IPDC if you can have access to anonymized data or if you can interview staff.
*   *Goal:* Ensure you won't be stopped later due to "confidentiality" issues.

### Step 7: Define the "Expected Outcomes"
What will the degree board and IPDC get at the end?
*   *Examples:* A new software architecture, a training manual for officers, or a decision-support dashboard.

### Step 8: Create a Realistic Timeline
Most Master’s theses take 5-6 months.
*   *Month 1:* Literature Review & Guideline design.
*   *Month 2-3:* Data collection (Interviews/Coding).
*   *Month 4:* Analysis.
*   *Month 5-6:* Writing & Revisions.

### Step 9: Format to XAMK/Academic Standards
*   Use **AMK/APA style** for citations.
*   Make sure your heading hierarchy is clear (Heading 1, 2, 3).
*   Ensure the English is formal and technical.

### Step 10: Feedback and Submission
*   *Action:* Send a draft to your supervisor *before* the final deadline. Ask for "Major Red Flags."
*   *Action:* Once approved by the supervisor, send a summary to IPDC Management to show them the value you are adding.

---

## Pro-Tips for Digital Industry Students:
1.  **Don't over-promise:** You don't need to build a perfect AI. You need to *research* how a perfect AI would work.
2.  **Focus on "Process":** Since your degree is in *Digital Industry*, always mention how technology changes the **manufacturing/industrial flow** of the bank's work.
3.  **Use Visuals:** Include a flowchart of the "Current Process" vs. the "AI-Enhanced Process."
